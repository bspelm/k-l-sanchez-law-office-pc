Understanding Workers' Compensation Laws in New York

The purpose of workers' comp, or workers' comp, is to pay medical bills and lost wages if an employee gets hurt or sick on the job. This is a mandatory insurance policy that every employer in the state of New York must carry. In exchange for being covered by this policy, employees give up their right to sue their employer for negligence in a personal injury case. If you're in Queens and find yourself needing assistance, a workers' compensation lawyer Queens can guide you through the process.

Almost all workers in New York are eligible for benefits from the state's Workers’ Compensation Law if they get hurt or sick at work. This includes part-time and seasonal workers, as well as independent contractors. The only groups of people who do not qualify for workers' comp are uniformed police, fire and sanitation personnel in the city, teachers, seamen and longshoremen, and federal government employees. Filing for Workers' Compensation in New York

While New York’s pioneering workers’ compensation laws were a step in the right direction, even today it is still not enough to cover all medical expenses and financial losses. The system can be very complex and navigating it correctly requires the help of an experienced workers' compensation lawyer Queens who understands the law and how it applies in your particular case.

One of the key elements to ensuring you get what you need is timely reporting your injury or illness to your employer and the appropriate authorities. If you do not meet the notification deadlines, your application for workers’ compensation will be denied. In most cases, you have two years to notify the New York State Workers’ Compensation Board and file a formal application for benefits. The key form that you will need to file is the Employee Claim Form C-3, which you can obtain from the New York State Workers’ Compensation Board or your employer. Consulting with a workers' compensation lawyer Queens can help ensure that all paperwork is filed correctly and on time.

It is critical to report your accident or work-related illness to your employer as soon as you can, and do so in person, if at all possible. This is not only a requirement by the workers’ comp law, but it will make it easier for your doctor to connect your condition to your job duties. It is also important for the insurance company to have as much information as possible about your claim. A workers' compensation lawyer Queens can assist in gathering and presenting this information effectively.

You will need to provide your doctor with a complete medical history of your injuries and illnesses. The doctor will then create a preliminary medical report that will be sent to the workers’ compensation board and your employer or their insurance provider. The doctor will also send a copy to you and your attorney. Having a workers' compensation lawyer Queens during this phase can provide additional oversight and ensure that all medical documentation supports your claim.

If your claim is accepted by the workers’ compensation board, they will send you an Administrative Decision or a Proposed Decision with their findings and award you the benefits that they believe you are entitled to. If you disagree with the decision, you have the right to request a hearing before an administrative law judge (ALJ). A workers' compensation lawyer Queens can represent you at the hearing, presenting your case and arguing on your behalf for the maximum possible benefits.

The ALJ will review your case and make a final decision that could award you more benefits or less. If you disagree with the ALJ’s decision, you have the right to appeal it to a panel of three members of the board. Finally, if you are not satisfied with the outcome of your appeal, you can take the case to the Supreme Court of New York. A workers' compensation lawyer Queens is essential throughout this appeals process, providing legal skills and advocating for your rights every step of the way.
